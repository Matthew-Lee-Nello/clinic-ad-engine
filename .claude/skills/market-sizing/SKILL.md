# market-sizing

Answer Daniel's standing question, "what is the TAM size?", rigorously and the same way every time. Output to brief section 3. Read `knowledge/tam-methodology.md` in full first, then brief sections 1 and 2.

## Do this

Follow `knowledge/tam-methodology.md` exactly:

1. **Top-down TAM** - population (target age/gender) × demand/prevalence × affordability share × act rate × price. Write every factor, its value, and its source or stated assumption.
2. **SAM** - narrow TAM to the reachable slice (geography, language, this clinic's price tier).
3. **SOM** - realistically winnable in 12 months.
4. **Bottom-up cross-check** - from the clinic's own budget, CPL, close rate, and price. Compare to top-down SOM; explain the gap (it is information: demand vs budget vs delivery constraint).
5. **Verdict** - one line: is this treatment worth launching now at the current budget, and what is the binding constraint.

## Rules

- Never invent a number. Every figure is sourced or a clearly labelled assumption. Show the working.
- Prefer the clinic's real numbers (price, CPL, close rate) over generic benchmarks.
- A defensible estimate with explicit assumptions is the goal, not false precision.
- Write section 3 of the brief (with the factor tables) and stop.
