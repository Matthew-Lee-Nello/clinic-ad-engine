# treatment-research

Understand the treatment the way a patient experiences it, and the way the market talks about it. Output to brief section 2. Read brief section 1 (client core) first.

## What to produce

Using web research (Exa/Tavily) plus the client profile:

- **What the treatment is**, in plain patient language, and the honest picture (what it does, who it suits, who it does not, recovery, real risks).
- **The patient journey:** trigger (what makes someone start looking) → research phase → the decision and what tips it → the fear that stops them.
- **The real questions and objections** the market actually asks: cost, downtime, pain, safety, longevity of results, "will it work for someone like me". Pull these from search suggestions, forums, Q&A sites, competitor FAQs. Quote the recurring ones.
- **Country-specific nuances** for this treatment: cultural attitudes, what is normalised vs taboo, seasonality, local terminology and language.

## Why this matters

The 25-30 angles later come from real market questions and real objections, matched to the doctor's point of difference. Thin treatment research produces generic angles. This stage is where specificity is won.

## Rules

- Cite where a claim or a recurring question comes from. Do not fabricate what "people ask".
- Keep it specific to the treatment AND the country; a treatment behaves differently market to market (Daniel's point).
- Write section 2 of the brief and stop.
