# scripts-and-copy

Two outputs, both to `clients/<slug>/03-copy.md`. Read `02-angles.md`, `knowledge/haynes-method.md`, and `knowledge/singapore-ad-compliance.md` first.

## Part 1: the shared copy pool

Per the Haynes method, you write **5 headlines and 5 body copies**, ONE shared set used across all 25 to 30 ads. Not one set per ad. They must be broad enough to sit under any of the angles.

- **5 headlines.** Short, factual, no superlatives, no results claims, no soliciting. Educational or curiosity-led framing. Under about 40 characters where possible for Meta.
- **5 body copies.** Each 50 to 150 words. Lead with the problem or a real question, explain plainly, invite the right person to a consultation softly (no pressure, no "book now for treatment" hard sell). No testimonials, no before/afters described, no comparisons.

Run every line through the compliance gate in `knowledge/singapore-ad-compliance.md`.

## Part 2: a video script per angle

For each angle marked video or both, write a short script for the doctor to film (talking-head, phone or simple setup):

- 30 to 60 seconds, spoken in the doctor's voice.
- Structure: hook (a real question or the problem) -> the mechanism or the honest fact -> what it involves -> soft invitation to learn more.
- Factual, educational, no superlatives, no results claims, no testimonials, no "guaranteed".
- Note any b-roll or on-screen text needed (compliant text only).

Keep scripts tight. The doctor is busy, and long scripts do not get filmed. Jeremy's own note: the bottleneck is filming speed, so make the doctor's job as small as possible.

## Output (`03-copy.md`)

- The 5 headlines and 5 body copies (the shared pool), each tagged compliant.
- A video script per video angle, numbered to match `02-angles.md`.
- A short note listing anything you softened or cut for compliance.
