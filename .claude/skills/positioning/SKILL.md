# positioning

Where is the market weak, and where does this doctor win. Output to brief section 5. Read brief sections 1-4 and consult the Schwartz advisor (`copywriter-schwartz`).

## Do this

1. **Market gaps.** From the competitor scan and treatment research: where are competitors generic, weak, samey, or non-compliant? What questions is nobody answering well? Where is the market underserved.
2. **The doctor's leverage.** Map the point of difference (brief section 1) against those gaps. Where the POD meets a gap the market actually cares about is the wedge. Daniel's exact aim: "leverage our client's strong points and fill in the gaps of the market."
3. **Buyer-spectrum diagnosis** (via `copywriter-schwartz`, grounded in `knowledge/breakthrough-advertising/`): what awareness stage is the prospect at, what sophistication stage is the market at, and therefore claim-led vs mechanism-led messaging. Crowded aesthetic markets are usually Problem/Solution-Aware at Stage 3-4 → lead with the doctor's mechanism and process.
4. **The positioning line.** One clear, compliant way to frame this clinic + treatment that sits on the wedge.

## Rules

- The wedge must be real: grounded in a substantiable POD and an actual market gap, not a slogan.
- Everything compliant (`knowledge/singapore-ad-compliance.md`).
- Write section 5 and stop.
