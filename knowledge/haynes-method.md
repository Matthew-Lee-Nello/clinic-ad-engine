# The Jeremy Haynes method (current, post-Andromeda)

The verified method for running Meta ads at scale, drawn from Jeremy Haynes' own material and checked against his corpus. This is the spine of the engine. When in doubt, follow this file.

## Core idea: messaging diversification, not variation testing

Under Meta Andromeda, targeting is controlled by messaging, not by ad-set settings. So the game is to give the system many genuinely different messages and let it find the winners.

- Launch **25 to 30 unique ads** per campaign. Each ad is one distinct reason to convert, filmed or designed through and through.
- Do NOT take a hook and paste it onto the same body and call to action. That is variation testing, and it is the old way.
- Alongside the 25 to 30 ads, run **5 headlines and 5 body-copy pieces**, the same set across every ad, written broad enough to apply to all of them. Meta pairs them dynamically.

Video and image ads live in the **same** pool of 25 to 30. Do not split them into separate campaigns. Let the system decide.

## Campaign structure

- **One cold campaign.** Warm-audience campaigns run only if the warm audience is large enough to be worth it, and then on a small budget.
- **Three ad sets inside the cold campaign:**
  - Broad (no interest targeting).
  - An interest stack (many interests, behaviours, and demographic traits loaded into one ad set, not separated out).
  - A lookalike stack (1% lookalikes built from highest-value customers, all customers, leads who showed up to calls, qualified leads who booked).
- All three ad sets contain the **same** 25 to 30 ads and the **same** 5 plus 5 copy.
- **Ad-set-level budgets (ABO), not CBO.** This lets you control distribution across the ad sets.
- Turn **off** Advantage+ creative auto-features and site links. The one exception worth keeping is the setting that highlights a positive comment.

## What happens after launch

- **1 to 3 ads get almost all the reach.** The other 27 to 29 spend pennies. This is by design. The belief that every creative deserves distribution is outdated. Forcing spend onto all of them only inflates cost per result.
- Do not panic that most ads got no reach. They are spare inventory, not failures.

## The scaling loop (if this, then that)

1. When 1 to 3 winners appear, scale that winning combination.
2. Scale until you hit a **ceiling**: above it, cost per result climbs and volume drops. Pull spend back down to the ceiling. That ad set becomes a **foundational campaign** or foundational ad set.
3. **Duplicate the winning ad set.** In the duplicate:
   - Keep the 27 to 29 ads that got no reach.
   - Pull out the 1 to 3 winners (they graduate to the scaling or foundational campaign).
   - Add 1 to 3 fresh concepts.
   - You are back to a full set of 25 to 30 at test budget. Repeat.
4. **Lead-quality gate.** If the winners bring poor leads (the sales team or setter says the leads are bad), treat them as losers even though they technically won on cost. Kill that messaging and avoid that angle. Duplicate and move on.
5. Keep spare ads on the side so you can relaunch fast when an ad set fatigues, without filming from scratch.

## The ad versus the landing page

- The landing page or VSL does the heavy selling and nurturing.
- But the ad's message still matters, because messaging controls who you attract, which drives lead quality. The ad does not need heavy production, but each of the 25 to 30 needs a sharp, specific reason to convert.

## The buyer spectrum (how to generate angles)

Built on Eugene Schwartz's Breakthrough Advertising, awareness and sophistication:

- **Early or unaware market:** a simple, direct claim converts.
- **More sophisticated or aware market:** you have to introduce a unique mechanism and talk about process to get probable buyers to act.
- The spectrum also spans buyer risk tolerance and willingness to pay.

Use the spectrum to spread the 25 to 30 angles across different levels of awareness and sophistication, anchored to the doctor's real point of difference.

## Budget reality

The method assumes real daily spend. At a low daily budget the 1 to 3 winners still emerge, but the signal is slower and noisier. So at low budget, widen the read window before you judge or scale, or run fewer ad sets so each gets more budget. There is no published minimum spend per creative, so do not invent one.

## What Jeremy does NOT teach

- He does not teach swiping "winning ads" from the ad library and copying them. His engine is generate-from-first-principles: 25 to 30 unique reasons to convert off the buyer spectrum and the client's point of difference. Use library research for structure and format inspiration, not as a source of angles to copy.
- A "winner" is decided after launch: the ad that earns the reach and passes the lead-quality gate. Not the longest-running ad in a library.
