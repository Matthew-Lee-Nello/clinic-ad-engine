# Hormozi primer (always read this first)

Distilled Alex Hormozi frameworks for offer, value, and lead gen. Read this every time you consult the Hormozi advisor. Go to the full corpus in this folder (`100m-offers.md`, `100m-leads.md`, `100m-money-models.md`, `organic-content-101...md`) only when you need depth on a specific mechanism.

## The Value Equation (the core lens)

Perceived value goes up when you move these four levers:

```
Value = (Dream Outcome  ×  Perceived Likelihood of Achievement)
        ---------------------------------------------------------
        (Time Delay  ×  Effort & Sacrifice)
```

- **Dream Outcome** (raise): the result they actually want, painted specifically.
- **Perceived Likelihood of Achievement** (raise): proof, mechanism, guarantees, specificity, credibility that it will work for them.
- **Time Delay** (lower): how fast they see a result. Faster feels more valuable.
- **Effort & Sacrifice** (lower): how little they must do or give up. Easier feels more valuable.

Every angle and hook should push at least one lever. The strongest push likelihood-up and effort-down at the same time.

## The Grand Slam Offer

An offer so good people feel stupid saying no. Build it by:
1. List the dream outcome.
2. List every problem/obstacle between them and it.
3. Turn each problem into a solution (a deliverable).
4. Trim to the highest-value, lowest-cost-to-deliver set.
5. Enhance with: scarcity (limited slots), urgency (a deadline/cohort), guarantees (reverse the risk), and naming (a clear, benefit-led name).

## Guarantees (reverse the risk)

Unconditional, conditional, anti-guarantee, or implied. The bolder the guarantee you can honestly stand behind, the more you raise perceived likelihood. In regulated niches, a guarantee about a medical result is off-limits, so shift the risk reversal to the *process* (the consultation, the assessment, the experience), never the clinical outcome.

## Lead gen: the Core Four

Ways to get anyone to know you exist: warm outreach, cold outreach, post free content, run paid ads. Paid ads (our lane) win on a strong hook + a specific dream outcome + an easy next step.

## Hooks and attention

- Lead with the prospect's problem or desire, stated specifically, in the first line.
- Specificity beats hype: real numbers, real detail, real mechanism.
- Curiosity + a concrete benefit pulls the click.

## Voice

Direct, blunt, concrete. Short sentences. Real numbers. No fluff, no hype words. Says the obvious thing plainly. Cares about what makes money, not about sounding clever.

## How to apply here (clinics)

- Sharpen each of the 25 to 30 angles: which value-equation lever does it push? If none, cut it.
- Strengthen hooks: is the first line a specific problem or dream outcome for the right person?
- Frame the consultation offer as a risk-reversed, easy, fast next step, without promising a clinical result and without breaching the compliance rail (`knowledge/singapore-ad-compliance.md`).
