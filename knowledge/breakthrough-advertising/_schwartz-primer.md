# Breakthrough Advertising primer (always read this first)

Distilled Eugene Schwartz, Breakthrough Advertising. This is the copy engine for the whole system. Read this every time you write or judge copy or angles. The full book is in this folder (`breakthrough-advertising.md`) for depth on any point.

## The one big idea

You cannot create desire. You can only channel the mass desire that already exists in the market onto your product. The copywriter's job is to recognise that desire and connect it to the treatment. So the work is diagnosis first: what does this market already want, and how aware and sophisticated are they.

## The five stages of market AWARENESS

How much the prospect already knows. This sets what the copy must open with.

1. **Most Aware** - knows the product and wants it. Copy: the offer and the terms. (Rare in cold ads.)
2. **Product-Aware** - knows the product exists but is not sure it is for them. Copy: reinforce desire, sharpen the differences, prove the claim, reduce risk.
3. **Solution-Aware** - knows the result they want and that solutions exist, but not your product. Copy: lead with the specific outcome, then introduce your mechanism as the way to get it.
4. **Problem-Aware** - feels the problem, does not know a solution exists. Copy: name the problem and the feeling sharply, then reveal that a solution exists.
5. **Unaware** - does not know they have the problem, or will not admit it. Copy: start with the world they live in, a truth or a story, and lead them to the problem. The hardest and often the most educational.

Most compliant medical/aesthetic cold traffic sits at **Problem-Aware** and **Solution-Aware**. That is where the 25 to 30 angles should cluster, with a few Unaware (pure education) and a few Product-Aware (the doctor's specific mechanism).

## The five stages of market SOPHISTICATION

How many similar claims the market has already heard. This sets how hard you must work the claim.

1. **Stage 1 - first to market.** Be simple and direct. State the claim plainly. It works because nobody has heard it.
2. **Stage 2 - competitors copy the claim.** Enlarge it. Push the same claim further, bigger, more.
3. **Stage 3 - the market stops believing bigger claims.** Introduce a **new mechanism**: the how, the reason it works. This is the turning point most treatments live at.
4. **Stage 4 - mechanisms get copied.** Elaborate and out-mechanism: a better, more specific, more elegant mechanism than the competitors.
5. **Stage 5 - the market is exhausted and cynical.** Shift from the claim to **identification and experience**: who this is for, the feeling, the identity, the brand. Bring the prospect into it.

Diagnose where the treatment sits in the target country (from `01-research.md`). Crowded aesthetic markets are usually **Stage 3 to 4**, so lead with the doctor's specific mechanism and process, not a bare claim. This is exactly why compliant, mechanism-led copy wins here.

## The techniques that carry the copy

- **Gradualisation:** build belief step by step; do not ask for the big leap on line one.
- **Redefinition:** reframe what the treatment or the problem *means* to shift how it is valued.
- **Mechanisation:** make the mechanism vivid and concrete so the claim becomes believable.
- **Concentration / camouflage / etc.** are in the book; reach for them when a specific angle needs it.

## Headline job

The headline's only job is to stop the right person and pull them into the first line of body copy, matched to their awareness stage. Not to sell, not to be clever. In our compliant lane: factual, specific, curiosity or problem-led, no superlatives, no results claims.

## How to apply here (clinics)

- For every angle, name its **awareness stage** and the market's **sophistication stage**, then write to match.
- Default posture for crowded aesthetic markets: Problem/Solution-Aware prospect, Stage 3 to 4 sophistication → lead with the mechanism and process.
- Everything still passes the compliance rail (`knowledge/singapore-ad-compliance.md`). Schwartz makes the copy persuasive; compliance decides what is allowed. Compliance always wins.
